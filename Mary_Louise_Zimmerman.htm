Mary Louise Zimmerman Nutley

  • Born: December 13, 1931, in Vancouver, BC
  • Died: April 3, 2004, Redding, CA
  • Married: Eugene Arthur Nutley in 1953

Born in Vancouver BC, Mary moved with her family to Seattle Washington in 1941. She was a chemistry major at the University of Washington, where she met her future husband on a ski team trip. She married Eugene Arthur Nutley in 1953, and had their first child in Seattle in 1955. The family lived in Florida and different locations in California before settling in Redding California in 1962. She had another son while living in San Francisco in 1962, and two more sons in Redding, born in 1964 and 1966.

Mary Lou helped initiate Shasta Friends, a chapter of Big Brothers/Big Sisters Inc., and served as its first president. She was the first woman president of the Shasta County United Way. She volunteered for HELPline, a telephone crisis intervention and referral service. She was the 1986 Citizen of the Year.

Mary Lou was an active participant in the League of Women Voters, the American Association of University Women and a volunteer for the Redding Museum of Art and History (before it became Turtle Bay). She served for 16 years on the Shasta Union High School District board and was a Shasta County YMCA board member.

Mary Lou co-owned a Redding travel agency for some years, and traveled all over the world. She and her husband took a boat trip up the Yangtze River in China, counted birds for a birding group in New Zealand, visited Iceland, and different destinations in Europe and Japan. The couple went to Antartica, twice.

She was an avid photographer, developing her photos in a home darkroom.

She was a supporter of the local independent art film movie theater, the restored Cascade in downtown Redding. After her death, a group of her friends thought to honor her memory with a seat sponsorship at the theater and a plaque. They asked for public donations, and received enough money for 8 seats.
