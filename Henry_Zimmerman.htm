Henry Eugene Zimmerman

  • Born: 1890 in Everett, WA
  • Died: 1955 in Seattle, WA
  • Married: Gertrude Mary Thompson in 1917

He attended the University of Washington until entering the military in 1917 for the First World War. He was sent to France shortly before the armistice was declared in 1918. He married Gertrude Mary Thompson just before being posted to Europe. While at the University of Washington, he was stroke on their first crew to race in the Poughkeepsie regatta in 1913. He was captain of the UW crew in 1914 which also raced in Poughkeepsie.

His career was with the J.H. Baxter Co. of San Francisco, producing telephone poles. He was head of their Canadian branch in Vancouver, B.C. for seventeen years beginning in 1924. He scouted the area for appropriate timber and managed the logs through harvesting and shipping in rafts to Seattle where they were sold.

His daughter was born in Vancouver in 1931. In 1941 the family moved to Seattle, Washington. He lived to greet his first grandson in 1955. He loved to take yearly fishing and hunting trips with his brothers-in-law.
